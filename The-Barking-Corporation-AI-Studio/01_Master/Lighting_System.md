# Lighting System

Version: 2.0

Project: The Barking Corporation

Status: Production Ready

---

# Purpose

This document defines the lighting standards for The Barking Corporation.

Every environment, character, and scene must follow these lighting guidelines to maintain a consistent visual identity across all episodes.

Lighting should enhance storytelling, guide viewer attention, and reinforce the mood of the scene without distracting from the characters.

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Lighting should feel:

• Natural
• Soft
• Cinematic
• Warm
• Clean
• Consistent
• Professional

The office should always feel welcoming and lived-in.

Avoid harsh or dramatic lighting unless the story specifically requires it.

---

# Studio Lighting Principles

Every scene should contain:

✓ Key Light

✓ Fill Light

✓ Rim Light

✓ Ambient Light

✓ Practical Lights

The goal is to create depth while keeping characters clearly visible.

# Character Lighting

Characters remain the brightest subject in every frame.

Priority

Face

Eyes

Upper body

Hands

Dialogue should never occur in shadow.

---

# Eye Lighting

Eyes should always contain catchlights.

Purpose

Bring life to characters.

Create emotional connection.

Avoid dull or lifeless eyes.

---

# Fur Rendering

Fur should display:

Soft highlights

Natural texture

Controlled reflections

Subtle rim lighting

Avoid overly shiny fur.

---

# Shadow Rules

# Exposure Rules

Characters should always be properly exposed.

Highlights must retain detail.

Backgrounds should support—not overpower—the subject.

Avoid blown-out windows.

Avoid crushed blacks.

---

# Background Lighting

Backgrounds should feel alive.

Include

Monitor glow

Window light

Hallway light spill

Desk lamps

Plants with natural highlights

Subtle reflections

Never distract from the main subject.

# Continuity Rules

Lighting direction must remain consistent across scene cuts.

If sunlight enters from the left in Scene 1,

it should continue from the left in Scene 2 unless the story indicates a location or time change.

# Studio Standards

Lighting should support the story—not become the story.

The audience should immediately recognize the world of The Barking Corporation from its warm, consistent office lighting.

Every episode should feel as if it was filmed in the same office building, regardless of the room or story.

Consistency creates immersion.
