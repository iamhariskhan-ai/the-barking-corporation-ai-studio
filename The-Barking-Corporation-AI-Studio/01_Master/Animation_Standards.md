# Animation Standards

Version: 2.0

Project: The Barking Corporation

Status: Production Ready

---

# Purpose

This document defines the animation quality standards for The Barking Corporation.

Every generated scene must follow these standards to ensure consistency across all episodes, regardless of the AI model or rendering platform.

These standards apply to:

• Character Animation
• Facial Animation
• Body Animation
• Eye Movement
• Gestures
• Camera Interaction
• Environmental Animation
• Background Animation

---

# Animation Philosophy

Animation should feel:

Natural

Expressive

Appealing

Believable

Stylized

Cinematic

Characters should never move like robots.

Every movement must have purpose.

Animation should communicate personality before dialogue.

---

# Animation Style

Target Style

Pixar-inspired stylized 3D animation.

Smooth animation.

Rounded motion.

Soft anticipation.

Natural follow-through.

High-quality interpolation.

No jerky movement.

No robotic poses.

---

# Animation Principles

Every animation follows the classical animation principles.

## Timing

Movement should feel natural.

Fast actions:

200–500 ms

Normal actions:

500–1200 ms

Slow actions:

1200–2500 ms

Avoid instant movements.

---

## Ease In / Ease Out

Every movement starts slowly.

Accelerates naturally.

Stops smoothly.

Avoid linear motion.

---

## Anticipation

Before every major action:

Small preparation movement.

Examples

Before standing

Slight lean forward

Before pointing

Small shoulder movement

Before walking

Weight shift

---

## Follow Through

Nothing stops instantly.

Tail

Ears

Clothing

Hands

Coffee mug

ID card

Continue moving naturally.

---

## Overlapping Action

Multiple body parts move independently.

Example

Buddy waves

↓

Arm moves

↓

Head follows

↓

Tail wags

↓

Body shifts

Movement should never feel rigid.

---

Characters always maintain:

Natural posture

Balanced weight

Relaxed shoulders

Slight breathing

Micro head movement

Natural blinking

Subtle tail movement

Even when standing still.

Finger movement

Coffee sip

Looking around

These should remain subtle.

# Running

Running should only occur when justified.

Examples

Late for meeting

Coffee emergency

Server outage

# Facial Animation

Faces should carry most of the emotion.

Use:

Eyes

Eyebrows

Mouth

Cheeks

Head tilt

Ear movement

Tail movement

Expressions should feel readable without dialogue.

---

# Eye Animation

Eyes should:

Track the speaking character.

Maintain believable eye contact.

Blink naturally.

Look away while thinking.

Avoid constant staring.

---

# Blink Timing

Natural blinking

Every 3–6 seconds

Fast double blink

Surprise

Long blink

Thinking

Half blink

Confusion

---

# Mouth Animation

Lip movement should approximately match dialogue.

Mouth should never remain open after speaking.

Avoid exaggerated mouth stretching.

Maintain species-specific facial proportions.

---

# Hand Gestures

Gestures should reinforce dialogue.

Examples

Explaining

Open hands

Thinking

Touch chin

Greeting

Small wave

Pointing

Single finger

Agreement

Small nod

Avoid excessive gesturing.

Examples

Coffee mugs

Laptops

Keyboard

Mouse

Doors

Chairs

Whiteboards

# Sitting

Characters should:

Shift weight

Place hands naturally

Always include:

Breathing

Weight shifting

Eye movement

Blinking

# Typing Animation

Typing should feel authentic.

Finger movement varies.

Occasional mouse interaction.

# Meeting Behaviour

Characters should:

Nod

Take notes

0.3–0.6 sec

0.5–1 sec

1 sec

# Gemini Veo Notes

When generating scenes:

• Prefer natural motion over exaggerated action.
• Maintain character proportions across scenes.
• Keep gestures readable.
• Use idle animation continuously.
• Prioritize facial expressions during dialogue.
• Ensure object interaction follows believable physics.
• Avoid sudden changes in animation style between scenes.
