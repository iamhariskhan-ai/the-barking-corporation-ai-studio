Version: 2.0

Project: The Barking Corporation

# Purpose

This document defines the global production standards for The Barking Corporation.

Every episode, scene, character, environment, camera setup, animation, and dialogue must follow these standards to maintain a consistent visual identity and storytelling style.

This document acts as the "operating system" for the studio.

---

# Studio Identity

The Barking Corporation is a modern animated workplace comedy featuring anthropomorphic animal employees working inside a technology company.

The world combines Pixar-inspired visual storytelling with relatable corporate life, software engineering culture, and light-hearted office humor.

Every episode should feel like it belongs to the same universe.

---

# Core Principles

## Consistency

Characters never randomly change appearance, clothing, personality, or voice.

Every episode should look like the previous episode.

---

## Simplicity

One episode.

One story.

One problem.

One punchline.

Avoid unnecessary complexity.

---

## Reusability

Every asset should be reusable.

Characters.

Props.

Animations.

Environments.

Lighting.

Camera presets.

Audio presets.

---

## Scalability

Design the production pipeline so hundreds of episodes can be produced without changing the overall quality.

---

## Family Friendly

Content should be suitable for viewers aged 13+.

Avoid offensive language.

Avoid politics.

Avoid religion.

Avoid violence.

Avoid adult themes.

---

# Animation Style

Pixar-inspired stylized 3D animation.

Soft cinematic lighting.

Large expressive eyes.

Natural fur rendering.

Rounded shapes.

Warm color palette.

Friendly facial expressions.

Natural body language.

Professional rendering quality.

Every shot should appear polished and cinematic.

# Character Rules

Characters must remain visually identical across every episode.

Do not modify:

Body proportions

Height

Clothing

Accessories

Voice

Personality

Relationships

Office role

Every character should be recognizable within one second.

# Camera Philosophy

Camera movement should support storytelling.

Preferred shots:

Wide

Medium

Close-up

Over-the-shoulder

Tracking

Reaction

Push-in

Avoid excessive movement.

Avoid unnecessary zooming.

Maintain visual clarity.

---

# Lighting Philosophy

Use realistic office lighting.

Warm morning light.

Neutral daytime lighting.

Soft evening lighting.

Consistent exposure.

No dramatic horror lighting.

Lighting should enhance emotion without distracting from the story.

---

# Audio Philosophy

Dialogue is always the highest priority.

Supporting audio:

Office ambience

Typing

Mouse clicks

Coffee machine

Slack notifications

Teams notifications

Chair movement

Door opening

Background audio should remain subtle.

---

# Dialogue Standards

Dialogue should be:

Short

Natural

Professional

Funny

Easy to understand

Avoid long speeches.

Characters should speak according to their personality.

---

Corporate culture
