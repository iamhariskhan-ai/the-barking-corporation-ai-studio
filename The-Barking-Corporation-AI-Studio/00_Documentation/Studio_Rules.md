# Studio Rules

Version: 1.0

Project:
The Barking Corporation

Purpose:
These rules define the mandatory standards for every episode produced by the studio. Every AI-generated asset, script, animation, and prompt must follow these rules.

---

# Rule 1 — Brand Consistency

Every episode must look like it belongs to the same universe.

Characters, environments, lighting, animation quality, and humor should remain consistent.

Never drastically change the art style.

---

# Rule 2 — Character Consistency

Characters never change:

• Species
• Name
• Personality
• Clothing
• Voice
• Office role
• Relationships
• Body proportions

Characters should be immediately recognizable.

---

# Rule 3 — Story Simplicity

Every episode focuses on one central idea.

Examples:

• Monday meeting
• Coffee machine broken
• Production bug
• Performance review
• Salary day
• Office party

Avoid multiple storylines.

---

# Rule 4 — Episode Length

Target runtime:

30–45 seconds

Recommended structure:

Hook: 5–7 sec

Conflict: 8–10 sec

Escalation: 8–10 sec

Resolution: 8 sec

Punchline: 5–8 sec

---

# Rule 5 — Family Friendly

Content must remain appropriate for all ages.

Avoid:

• Violence
• Politics
• Religion
• Adult jokes
• Offensive language
• Hate speech

---

# Rule 6 — Office Realism

Although characters are animals, the office environment behaves like a real software company.

Use relatable scenarios:

• Standups
• Sprint planning
• HR meetings
• Client calls
• Deployments
• Code reviews
• Performance reviews
• Slack messages
• Teams meetings

---

# Rule 7 — Visual Style

Pixar-inspired stylized 3D animation.

Bright colors.

Soft lighting.

Large expressive eyes.

Clean office interiors.

Modern technology company.

No realistic humans.

---

# Rule 8 — Camera Language

Avoid random camera movement.

Use intentional cinematic shots.

Preferred:

• Wide
• Medium
• Close-up
• Over-the-shoulder
• Tracking
• Push-in
• Reaction shots

---

# Rule 9 — Dialogue

Dialogue should be:

Short

Natural

Funny

Professional

Easy to understand

Never use long monologues.

---

# Rule 10 — Comedy

Comedy should come from:

Character personalities

Office situations

Misunderstandings

Corporate culture

Timing

Facial expressions

Avoid slapstick that feels unrelated.

---

# Rule 11 — Animation

Movement should feel natural.

Characters should:

Blink

Breathe

Shift weight

Use hand gestures

Move tails naturally

Avoid robotic movement.

---

# Rule 12 — Audio

Dialogue is always the loudest audio.

Background sounds support the scene.

Use realistic office ambience.

Avoid excessive music.

---

# Rule 13 — Scene Design

Each episode should use no more than:

1–2 locations

2–4 active characters

Minimal props

Simple transitions

---

# Rule 14 — Reusability

Every environment, character, and animation should be reusable.

Avoid creating one-off assets unless necessary.

---

# Rule 15 — Production Philosophy

Consistency > Complexity

Quality > Quantity

Simple > Confusing

Memorable > Fancy

Reusable > Disposable

---

# Studio Motto

Build once.

Reuse forever.

Tell simple stories beautifully.
