# Purpose

Defines the standard meeting behavior for all characters during stand-ups, sprint planning, brainstorming sessions, client meetings, design reviews, retrospectives, and executive discussions.

Meetings should feel collaborative, professional, and engaging while allowing every character to contribute naturally.

---

# Description

Characters gather around a meeting table or presentation area, actively listening, discussing ideas, taking notes, and interacting respectfully.

Movement should be subtle and focused rather than exaggerated.

---

# Body Movement

- Upright sitting or standing posture
- Slight forward lean while listening
- Relaxed shoulders
- Occasional posture adjustments
- Natural breathing
- Comfortable seated position

Avoid excessive movement or fidgeting.

---

# Head Movement

- Turn naturally toward the current speaker
- Gentle nods during agreement
- Small head tilts while listening
- Look toward presentation screen or whiteboard
- Brief eye contact with teammates

Head movement should follow the conversation naturally.

---

# Hand Movement

- Rest naturally on table
- Hold notebook or tablet
- Small hand gestures while speaking
- Take notes occasionally
- Point toward presentation when explaining
- Fold hands while listening

Avoid exaggerated arm movements.

---

# Eye Direction

Characters should naturally alternate focus between:

- Current speaker
- Presentation screen
- Whiteboard
- Notebook
- Laptop
- Other participants

Maintain active engagement throughout the meeting.

# Negative Prompt

Avoid:

- Chaotic discussions
- Everyone speaking simultaneously
- Exaggerated hand movements
- Looking in random directions
- Sleeping characters
- Distracted participants
- Poor posture
- Robotic movement
- Empty facial expressions
- Unrealistic meeting behavior
