# Episode Formula

Version: 1.0

Project: The Barking Corporation

Purpose:
Define the standard structure for every episode to maintain consistent pacing, storytelling, and audience engagement.

---

# Episode Length

Target Duration:
30–45 seconds

Ideal:
35 seconds

---

# Story Structure

## Scene 1 — Hook (0–3 sec)

Goal:
Grab attention immediately.

Examples:

- Production server is down.
- Buddy looks shocked.
- Percy starts a rumor.
- CEO walks into the engineering floor.
- Slack notification surprises everyone.

Rule:
Create curiosity within the first 3 seconds.

---

## Scene 2 — Setup (3–10 sec)

Introduce:

- Characters
- Problem
- Environment

Examples:

Ben accidentally pushes code on Friday.

Ruby notices a confusing UI.

Luna announces an unexpected policy.

---

## Scene 3 — Escalation (10–22 sec)

Increase tension.

Examples:

QA finds more bugs.

Slack keeps exploding.

Customer reports arrive.

Everyone starts discussing solutions.

Rule:
Raise the stakes without creating panic.

---

## Scene 4 — Resolution (22–35 sec)

Problem gets solved through teamwork.

Every department contributes.

Characters learn something.

Never rely on luck.

---

## Scene 5 — Ending (35–45 sec)

Finish with:

Funny joke

Unexpected twist

Character catchphrase

Light office moment

Question to audience

Example:

Frank:
"I found one more bug."

Everyone:
"Oh no..."

---

# Episode Rules

One primary problem.

Maximum 4 speaking characters.

One main location.

Clear beginning, middle, and end.

Family-friendly humor.

Professional workplace behavior.

End positively.

---

# Story Checklist

□ Strong hook

□ Clear problem

□ Character interaction

□ Comedy

□ Resolution

□ Positive ending

□ Natural dialogue

□ Visual storytelling
